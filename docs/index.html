Southern California City Classifier

Pushing past a 36% human baseline to achieve 93% validation accuracy using a fine-tuned MobileNetV3-Large architecture.

The Challenge

Classifying geographic locations based purely on street-level imagery is a notoriously difficult task. Many Southern California cities share identical biomes, infrastructure styles, and lighting conditions. To establish a baseline, a human test yielded only a 36% accuracy rate when attempting to distinguish between these six regions: Anaheim, Bakersfield, Los Angeles, Riverside, San Luis Obispo (SLO), and San Diego.

The Data & Preprocessing

The model was trained on a dataset of approximately 9,100 labeled street-view images. To prevent the network from overfitting on specific camera angles or times of day, aggressive data augmentation was applied to the training pipeline:

  • Random Horizontal Flips (50% probability)
  • Random Rotations (up to 15 degrees)
  • Color Jittering (adjusting brightness, contrast, and saturation)

Architecture & Training

The core engine of this project is a MobileNetV3-Large Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) pre-trained on ImageNet1K. This specific architecture was chosen to maximize feature extraction while keeping the final weights file under a strict 50MB constraint.

~5.4M Parameters
10 Epochs
0.001 Initial LR (Adam)

To ensure gentle convergence and prevent the model from overshooting the local minima in the final epochs, a StepLR scheduler was implemented, decaying the learning rate by 50% every 3 epochs.

Results

Evaluated against a completely unseen testing dataset, the model achieved a staggering 92.92% validation accuracy, thoroughly outperforming the human baseline and demonstrating a deep structural understanding of the subtle differences between SoCal cities.
